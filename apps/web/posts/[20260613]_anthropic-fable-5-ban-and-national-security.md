On June 12, 2026, the artificial intelligence company Anthropic announced that the US government issued an export control directive requiring the immediate suspension of access to two of its models, Fable 5 and Mythos 5. This suspension applies to all foreign nationals, including Anthropic's own employees, effectively forcing the company to disable the models for all customers to ensure compliance. Access to other models, such as the Opus and Sonnet series, remains unaffected.

The directive was issued citing national security authorities, though specific details of the underlying threat were not immediately disclosed.

## The Core Issue: Jailbreaking and Cybersecurity

According to Anthropic's public statement, the government's concern stems from a potential "jailbreak"—a method used to bypass the safety safeguards built into the AI model.

The reported jailbreak is considered "narrow" and "non-universal." Based on Anthropic's review of the incident, the technique involved prompting the model to read a specific software codebase and identify or fix software flaws within it.

Anthropic maintains a defense-in-depth strategy for its models, acknowledging that perfect jailbreak resistance is not currently possible across the industry. Their approach with Fable 5 was designed to ensure that any potential jailbreaks would either be highly specific (and therefore limited in utility) or computationally expensive to execute. The company also enforced a 30-day data retention policy specifically for Fable to monitor for and mitigate successful attacks.

## Industry Context and Capabilities

A central point of contention raised by Anthropic is the capability level of the exploit. The company stated that the vulnerability demonstrated in the government's report is relatively simple and that the level of capability displayed by Fable 5 under this jailbreak is widely available from other public models, including those from competitors.

Anthropic argues that using code-analysis capabilities to identify simple vulnerabilities is a standard function utilized daily by cybersecurity defenders to secure systems, rather than an exclusive offensive threat that warrants the suspension of a commercial model deployed to millions of users.

## Implications for AI Deployment

The immediate recall of Fable 5 and Mythos 5 represents a significant regulatory action in the AI sector. Anthropic has stated they are complying with the legal directive but disagree with the premise that a narrow jailbreak constitutes grounds for a mandatory recall.

The company warned that if this specific standard of vulnerability were applied uniformly across the artificial intelligence industry, it would likely halt all new frontier model deployments.

Anthropic has indicated they are working to clarify the misunderstanding with government officials and aim to restore access to the models. This event underscores the ongoing tension between rapid AI capability development and government oversight regarding national security and cybersecurity risks.
