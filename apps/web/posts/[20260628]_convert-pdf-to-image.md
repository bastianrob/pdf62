Because nothing is uploaded, the tool keeps working even when you're offline.

## PNG or JPEG? Choosing the Right Format

- **PNG** is lossless and keeps text and line art razor-sharp. Files are larger, but quality is perfect. Best for documents, diagrams, and screenshots you'll zoom into.
- **JPEG** produces much smaller files and is ideal for scanned pages and photo-heavy content. You can also dial in the quality to balance size against clarity.

## Picking the Right Resolution (DPI)

Resolution controls how detailed — and how large — your images are:

- **72–96 DPI (Screen):** Small files, great for on-screen previews and web use.
- **150 DPI (Standard):** A balanced default that looks clean almost everywhere.
- **300 DPI (Print):** Maximum sharpness for printing, at the cost of bigger files.

A quick tip: converting **many pages at 300 DPI** creates large images and takes longer, so if you only need a few pages, set a page range first. For everyday sharing, PNG at 150 DPI is a great place to start.
